01 — Economic Impact

Strengthening the local economy & reshaping competition

StepGuard's predictive model generates direct savings for operators, restructures the German maintenance market, and seeds local job creation.

Savings for operators

Public transport operators, airports and shopping centres benefit from lower downtime costs and fewer emergency call-outs — reinvestable directly into local transport infrastructure.

⚙️
OEM market disruption

Dominant OEMs (Schindler, KONE, ThyssenKrupp) earn large revenues from reactive maintenance. StepGuard compresses that cycle, incentivising the entire sector to innovate toward preventive models.

🏭
Local supply chain

StepGuard uses IDS cameras produced in Obersulm, Germany — directly supporting domestic hardware manufacturing and strengthening the German industrial supply chain.

👷
Job creation

Phase 3 scale-up will require a German service centre, generating direct local employment. Indirect effects include demand for data-literate maintenance technicians across the sector.

02 — Social Impact

Safer cities. Fairer workplaces. Inclusive infrastructure.

Escalator reliability is a public safety issue — especially for elderly and mobility-impaired users. StepGuard creates measurable social value at scale.

04 — Ethical Impact

Responsible AI. Accountable diffusion. Global principles.

From GDPR compliance to global expansion ethics, StepGuard is built on the principle that technology must earn trust — not assume it.

16 Peace, Justice & Strong Institutions
17 Partnerships for the Goals
85% of German companies identify data protection as their top concern in AI adoption — which is why privacy-by-design is non-negotiable for StepGuard. (Statista, 2024)
🔒

Privacy by design — GDPR & BDSG

StepGuard cameras monitor mechanical components only. A public-facing data processing statement and an independent GDPR audit are completed before any German deployment. No personal data is collected, processed or retained — ever.

🤖

Human oversight in AI decisions

The four-level alarm system recommends action — it does not autonomously enforce escalator shutdowns without operator confirmation. Auditability and human oversight are foundational design requirements, aligned with the EU AI Act's notified body assessment from January 2027.

🏢

Ethical competitive displacement

StepGuard does not aim to eliminate the reactive maintenance workforce. Cboost actively supports operators in managing workforce reskilling — and frames its messaging around improving safety conditions, not purely reducing labour costs.

🌐

Responsible global diffusion

As StepGuard expands into new European markets, Cboost commits to ethical minimum conditions in all regions: mandatory operator engagement with maintenance workforces and full compliance with local data protection law — not just where enforcement is strict.
